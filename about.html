HOW THE TECHNOLOGY WORKS

To understand how VR technology works to create an alternative reality, we must understand how humans perceive reality. We create a sense of reality through the use of our senses. What we can see, feel, and hear is what our mind perceives as real. Using this principle, VR developers can expertly create entirely new worlds specifically designed to deceive the senses.

Replacing what our senses absorb is fundamental to how VR technology works. It changes our physical environment and swaps it for a virtual one. The headset uses sensors to track head movements, allowing users to look around and interact with the virtual environment, as well as software to create your surroundings. All of this works together to create the feeling that you're fully interacting with a world that does not exist.

To be more specific, to allow users to explore the ocean depth using VR technology, VR developers have to simulate an environment that interacts with human senses, primarily vision and hearing. Through a VR headset, users can experience high-resolution visuals of the ocean and use motion controllers to track hand movements, allowing people to interact with objects or virtually swim. Coming along with this, VR software uses 3D modeling, and audio to render realistic of underwater environments, with natural sound and marine creatures. By combining audio, visuals, and motion tracking, users can experience it as if they were underwater. They can try to reach out to touch a virtual whale or shark, contemplate the view, and hear the bubbling sound surrounding them.

    • Social and ethical issues surrounding the use of the technology

    Social interactions

    Social interactions: VR can impact the user's mind and psychology, especially when they are completely immersed in the virtual experience. While using VR, users often become isolated from the world and easily forget about real-time. This can cause negative effects such as chronic stress, depression, irritability, and psychological stress. In addition, being immersed in the virtual world can also cause distraction and reduce users' work and study performance.

    Eye health

    Eye health: One of the worrying harmful effects of virtual reality glasses on humans is the impact on vision and eye health. During VR use, users are often exposed continuously to very bright screens, which can cause irritations for the eyes and headaches. Studies have shown that continuous exposure to blue light can increase the risk of vision problems, including blind spots, farsightedness, nearsightedness, and retinal diseases. Additionally, long-term exposure to the LED screen of virtual reality glasses can cause dry and burning eyes, since the eyes do not blink frequently when using VR.

    Privacy

    Privacy: There are privacy concerns about VR technology. When users enrol in virtual reality, data of their actions, motions, facial expressions, and behaviours may be collected and stored. This personal information may be used for advertisement and research purposes. Moreover, as personal information is shared in virtual environments, fraud and identity theft are prone to happen.

    • Hardware and software requirements to run the technology

    VR Headset

    VR Headset: The VR headset includes a head-mounted display (HMD) with a computer monitor built-in in each eye, audio systems, and straps to secure it to the user’s head. The headset also consists of sensors for tracking the movements of the user's head. The VR headset provides a unique visual experience by displaying objects in three dimensions with stereophonic sound. As it tracks the movements in real-time, therefore, it can adjust the surroundings accordingly, creating the illusion of the user's presence in the virtual world.

    Motion controllers

    Motion controllers: They are handheld devices with buttons, triggers, and sensors. They track the motion of users to provide their interaction in virtual reality through hand movements. The sensor systems detect the position and orientation of users, allowing them to interact and manipulate virtual objects.

    Computer

    Computer: VR requires a powerful computer setting to run the immersive 3D world and keep up with the user's movements. They have to have advanced graphics processing capabilities, strong CPU, and sufficient RAM. A suitable computer system will ensure a smooth and responsive VR experience for users.

    Accessories (Optional)

    Accessories (Optional): Haptic gloves, and input devices can enhance the realistic experience of users when interacting with virtual creatures.

    Software requirements:

  • VR platform software: It plays an important role in the interface between users and the virtual world. VR platform software controls VR applications, updates, and settings. Some examples of VR platform software are StreamVR, Oculus Rift Software, or PlayStation VR Home.
  • VR Application Software: They are software designed specifically for the virtual world. These applications consist of games, simulations, or educational content. VR applications run the VR platform and deliver immersive experiences. They make effective use of VR hardware to create surroundings, interactions, and scenarios in the virtual world.
  • Graphic Drivers: Up-to-date graphic drivers are crucial for the smoothness of VR performance. These drivers are specific to the Graphic Processing Unit in the PC of users to optimise it and ensure the quality of visual elements in VR applications.
  • Operating System: VR systems are compatible with specific operating systems like Windows, macOS,...These operating systems offer a foundational software environment for the VR platform and applications to run. They also control the resources and facilities communication between hardware and software.

    • Discuss the people who would be required to design and create the virtual world

    To bring the marine world to real life, teamwork efforts have to be made:

  • 3D Artists and Modellers: They are responsible for creating realistic models of underwater environments including marine life and coral reefs, as well as waves and bubbles when motions are captured.
  • Programmers and Developers: They are in charge of building the VR platform and code for the program. They would code for interactions, and implement physics and even artificial intelligence in response to a wide range of user’s movements.
  • Sound Designers and Musicians: They play an important role in making the experience in the virtual world more trustworthy by creating immersive soundscapes, from the gentle lapping of waves, the bubble of surroundings, to the haunting calls of whales, or the sand vibration when a crab tríe to run away and hide under the rock.
  • Oceanographers and Marine Biologists: They provide professions on marine life behaviour and ecosystems so that the accuracy of the underwater world would be ensured.
